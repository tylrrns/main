Fig. 1 gives spacial domain output for a Low-pass filter with Gaussian distribution σ = 60. The mitochrondria—originally orange/red/yellow—appear white.

Gaussian filtering is used to blur images and remove noise and detail. The Gaussian filter works by using the 2D Gaussian distribution as a point-spread function, attained by convolving the (discrete approximation of the) 2D Gaussian distribution function with the image, where σ is the standard deviation of the function.

- Gaussian filter is a non-uniform Low-pass filter
- Central pixels are weighted more than pixels on the periphery
- Larger values of σ produce a wider peak (greater blurring). With High-pass filtering, the opposite is true.
