Thomas Sully. The Passage of the Delaware, 1819
[Genl. Washington looks on as Col. Knox directs troop movement.]

Passage of the Delaware,
in Dec. 25 th, 1776

By the American Troops under the command of General Washington.

Boats were in readiness, and the Troops began to cross about sunset, but the force of the current, the sharpness of the frost, the darkness of the night, the Ice which made during the operation, and a high wind, rendered the passage of the River extremely difficult, and but for the stentorian lungs and extraordinary exertions of Colonel Knox, it could not have been effected in season to favor the enterprise.

"We want great men who, when fortune frowns, will not be discouraged."
